An 8-week home-based randomized controlled trial with 40 autistic children (aged 5–9) examining whether engagement with a commercial social robot (Qrobot) translates into improved human-directed social abilities. Results reveal a "handoff versus siloing" pattern: robot withdrawal promoted reorientation toward human social interaction, while continued access concentrated engagement within the child–robot dyad.

**Keywords:** Social Robots, Autism, Child–Robot Interaction, Withdrawal Study, Social Transfer, Randomized Controlled Trial
